Service 01

Mold Inspection

Before we recommend anything, we find out what's actually going on in your home. Our inspection process takes the guesswork out of it — with third-party lab results and a plain-language explanation of what we found.

What happens
We visit your home and conduct a multipoint visual inspection
We take air and surface samples from key areas throughout the home (we can also do ERMI testing)
Samples are sent to an independent, third-party laboratory for analysis
Results are returned within a few days with a clear, plain-language explanation
Our promise

Honest answers. Not a sales pitch. It's our job to educate you and enable you to make the best decision for your home and family — and that starts with knowing exactly what you're dealing with.

Service 02

MTN Scrub

Most people think mold is something you can see. The reality is that mold sources are microscopic — living on flat surfaces throughout your home where dust collects and mold colonies take root. We call these bio-nesting sites. Before we sanitize, we eliminate them.

What happens
Our team uses HEPA vacuums to thoroughly clean flat surfaces throughout your home — cabinet tops, window sills, door frames, and anywhere (within reach) bio-nesting occurs
Simultaneously, we run commercial-grade air scrubbers to pull airborne particles and debris from the environment
The result is a clean, prepared environment where the MTN Sanitization can work at its full potential
Why it matters

Sanitizing over bio-nesting sites is like painting over rust. The MTN Scrub ensures we're treating a clean surface — so the results last longer and work better.

Part of treatment package

The MTN Scrub is always performed as the first step of a complete treatment — never as a standalone service.

Coming soon: annual MTN Scrub + testing as a recurring maintenance option for past customers — to keep your home performing at its best year after year.

Equipment used

Commercial HEPA vacuums  ·  Air scrubbers  ·  Microfiber surface tools

Service 03

MTN Sanitization

Once the environment is prepped, MTN Sanitization — using the proprietary Pure Maintenance dry vapor process — reaches every corner, crevice, and surface that traditional remediation misses — every cubic foot of air, every square inch of surface area in your home.

What happens
The dry vapor is deployed throughout your entire home
It penetrates areas that are invisible to the eye and impossible to reach by hand
Mold, bacteria, and other biological contaminants are neutralized at the source
The process requires little to no demolition and is completed in a single day
Why the dry vapor process works

Traditional remediation focuses on what's visible — cutting out drywall, treating surface stains (when needed, we can do this too). But mold lives everywhere, not just where you can see it. The dry vapor process doesn't just treat the problem in front of you. It treats the whole home.

What About Traditional Remediation?

Sometimes you need both. We can help either way.

Traditional remediation — physical removal of affected materials — is sometimes necessary before or alongside treatment. We can handle minor drywall removal (up to approximately 10 square feet) as part of our process. For larger removal needs, we'll refer you to a trusted partner and coordinate seamlessly.

Our goal is always to recommend what's right for your situation, not what's most profitable for us.
