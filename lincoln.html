Why Lincoln homes are vulnerable to mold

Lincoln's blend of older University District homes, newer southwest developments, and a high percentage of rental properties creates a wide range of mold risk profiles. Older homes frequently have uninsulated crawl spaces and aging plumbing. Newer developments can suffer from grading and drainage issues. Lincoln also sits in a region with significant seasonal humidity that drives attic and basement condensation.

Common Questions

Mold remediation in Lincoln — answered

Straight answers to what Lincoln homeowners ask most.

Is mold common in Lincoln homes?

Yes. Lincoln sees the same humid continental climate patterns as the rest of eastern Nebraska, and the city's mix of older housing stock and newer construction creates diverse mold risk. University-area homes, rentals with deferred maintenance, and newer builds with drainage issues are among the most common cases we see.

How quickly can you respond in Lincoln?

MTN PUR serves the Lincoln metro and typically offers same-week scheduling for inspections across Lancaster County including Southwest Lincoln, the University area, and East Lincoln.

Does homeowner's insurance cover mold remediation?

Coverage depends on the cause. Mold from a sudden covered event like a burst pipe is often covered. Mold from long-term moisture typically is not. MTN PUR provides documentation to support your insurance claim.

What is dry vapor mold remediation?

MTN Sanitization dry vapor converts an advanced EPA-registered solution into microscopic particles that penetrate every surface, crack, and air duct — reaching spaces traditional remediation can't, with minimal demolition.
