Why Prevention Matters

The number one cause of mold in Midwest homes

It's not a leaky roof. It's not a flooded basement. While those things can certainly cause mold problems, the number one driver of mold in Midwest homes is something far more common — humidity.

Like us, mold needs moisture to survive. But it doesn't need much. Elevated humidity creates microscopic condensation on surfaces throughout your home, allowing mold to develop in ways that are often invisible to the eye — until it becomes a bigger problem.

Controlling humidity is the single most effective thing a Midwest homeowner can do to prevent mold.
Target Humidity Range
30–45%
Relative Humidity

If any area of your home regularly exceeds 50% relative humidity, it's time to take action. A quality dehumidifier in problem areas can make a significant difference.

First step

Use a digital hygrometer with multiple sensors to monitor relative humidity in different areas of your home — basement, main floor, bedrooms, and any areas that feel damp or musty. These are widely available and inexpensive.

Humidity Management

A simple framework for a healthier home

These four steps go a long way toward preventing mold and maintaining a healthy indoor environment year-round.

01

Measure Your Humidity

You can't manage what you don't measure. Use a digital hygrometer with multiple sensors to monitor relative humidity in different areas of your home — basement, main floor, bedrooms, and anywhere that tends to feel damp. Target 30–45% RH.

If any area regularly exceeds 50%, move to Step 4 and add a dehumidifier.
02

Optimize Your HVAC System

Your HVAC system is your first line of defense against humidity. When properly sized and configured, it does significant dehumidification automatically. Have a qualified technician evaluate system sizing, fan speed settings, drainage, and overall performance.

Oversized systems often don't dehumidify effectively — this is a common and overlooked problem.

→ We can connect you with a vetted HVAC partner

03

Control Moisture at the Source

Bathrooms are one of the most common sources of indoor humidity. Hot showers release significant moisture — without proper ventilation, it settles on surfaces and feeds mold. Make sure your bathroom fan works properly, run it during showers and for at least 30 minutes after, and consider a timer switch.

A timer switch makes this automatic — you don't have to remember.
04

Add a Dehumidifier Where Needed

If your HVAC system isn't keeping humidity below 50% — particularly in basements and lower levels — a quality dehumidifier can make a significant difference. Look for a unit that's appropriately sized for the space, has a built-in humidistat, and can drain continuously.

A quality dehumidifier is one of the most effective investments a Midwest homeowner can make for long-term indoor air quality.
Exterior Moisture

Keep water moving away from your home

Humidity inside the home is often driven by moisture outside it. A few exterior factors are worth paying close attention to — especially here in Nebraska.

Downspouts & Gutters

Make sure your downspouts are directing water well away from your foundation — at least four to six feet. Clogged or misdirected gutters are a surprisingly common contributor to basement moisture problems.

Landscaping & Grading

The ground around your home should slope away from the foundation, not toward it. If water tends to pool near your home after rain, that moisture is finding its way in.

→ We can connect you with a vetted landscaping partner

Basements & Lower Levels

Basements are particularly vulnerable to moisture intrusion. Proper waterproofing and vapor barriers can make a significant difference in keeping lower levels dry and your whole home healthier.

Our Partner Network

You don't have to figure this out alone

Prevention often requires more than education — it requires action. And some of that action is outside our lane. That's why we've built a network of vetted, trusted partners across the services that matter most for indoor air quality and moisture management.

HVAC

System evaluation, sizing, fan settings, and humidity optimization. Your HVAC is your first line of defense — it should be working properly.

Plumbing

Visible leaks get fixed. It's the invisible ones — slow drips inside walls, under slabs, or behind appliances — that quietly create mold problems over time.

Leak Detection

Periodic leak detection checks are a smart preventive measure, especially in older homes. We can connect you with specialists who find what's hidden.

Waterproofing

Basement waterproofing, vapor barriers, and drainage systems to keep moisture from entering your home in the first place.

Landscaping & Drainage

Grading, drainage solutions, and landscaping adjustments to keep water moving away from your foundation rather than toward it.
